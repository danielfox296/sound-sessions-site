It's the nearest familiar name, and if you've been to one you already know how to arrive: lie down, get warm, and rest. A Firstwater session runs seventy-five minutes over a composed bed with live instruments, opened and closed with ceremony, and I tune the rig the way a studio tunes a control room.
I open the night and close it, and offer a nudge here and there along the way, so it is guided in that sense. There is nothing to follow and nothing to get right; you lie down and rest, and that is the whole of it. If you have never meditated, that changes nothing about how you arrive.
You lie down under blankets in low light. There is a formal opening, seventy-five minutes of engineered sound played live over a composed bed, and a formal close. The sound is strong enough to feel in your chest and soft enough to rest inside. On the way in you draw a card with one line on it, and it is yours to keep. Some nights I offer one invitation, like a moment of meeting another person's eyes, and you hear about it beforehand, never as a surprise. Every invitation is optional. After, there is tea for anyone who wants to stay and an open door for anyone who doesn't.
