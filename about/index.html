About

Half my songs
were ruined.

I make music for a living, and have for a long time, which means the number of songs tangled up in my life is bigger than most people's. After a breakup I found out how many of them were tangled up in her. Something I had built myself would come on and hand me a memory I didn't ask for, and I'd end up turning off my own work in my own studio.

What got the songs back

It was learning what low sound does to a body once the body finally stops bracing, and I learned it the way producers learn most things: alone in a room, late, half by accident. For a while I treated it as a private trick. Eventually it seemed wrong not to build the room I had needed myself.

Sound Sessions is that room. Facilitated sound experiences in Denver, engineered with sub-bass and studio-grade production, held with formal ceremony, each one built around something worth putting down. Sessions are facilitated processes toward things people want; nobody promises you an outcome, because the moving-through is yours.

The craft

Most rooms sound cheap.
This one doesn't.

Nearly everything in this practice comes down to production quality: sub-bass you feel in your sternum, arcs engineered the way records are engineered, a room treated like a studio instead of a stage. That is the whole bet, and it is the part a professional producer can do that most practitioners cannot.
